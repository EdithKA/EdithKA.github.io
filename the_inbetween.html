About the project

A nightmare built from forgotten childhood

Irene wakes up trapped. After a car accident that leaves her in an induced coma, her mind constructs a nightmare world from the liminal spaces of her 2000s childhood — empty malls, dim video rental stores, fast-food restaurants after closing time. She must explore, solve puzzles, and survive the horrors born from her own memories before the corruption consumes her entirely.

The In-Between is a first-person survival horror game developed in Unity as my Bachelor's Final Project at Universitat Jaume I. The project merges retro PS1/PS2 aesthetics with psychological terror, liminal space atmosphere, and deep nostalgia for the early 2000s. I focused on all the programming systems and 3D object modeling, building everything from the character controller to enemy AI from scratch.

Systems

Built from scratch

◈
Character Controller
Full first-person movement with sprint stamina, camera bob animations, and raycast-based object interaction. All actions have synced arm animations.
◈
Retro Phone HUD
Life displayed as battery, sanity as signal bars — styled after a Nokia 3310. Inventory browsable from the in-game phone, keeping the screen clean and immersive.
◈
Enemy AI — Finite State Machine
Two enemy types (melee and ranged) powered by NavMesh Agent and A* pathfinding. States: Patrol → Chase → Attack. Detection via raycast line-of-sight from enemy "eyes". Sprites rotate to always face the player, replicating the DOOM billboard technique.
◈
Puzzle System
Centralised PuzzleManager tracks objective states across four areas: the fast-food restaurant, the nursery, the video rental store, and the cinema. Completing puzzles restores sanity, unlocks doors and advances the narrative.
◈
Sanity & Corruption
Sanity drains near enemies or from extended exploration. As it drops, the environment visually corrupts. Full sanity loss causes death. Objects tied to Irene's memories restore sanity and trigger inner monologue dialogue.
Enemy design

Creatures born from memory

Enemies use 2D sprites in a 3D environment — the same technique as classic DOOM. Two scripts (AngleToPlayer and EnemySpriteLook) rotate the sprite in real time so it always faces the player, maintaining the retro visual consistency.

Enemy AI finite state machine — Patrol · Chase · Attack
Melee enemy — high damage, short range
Ranged enemy — low damage, detects from afar
3D Art

Objects from another era

All interactive and decorative objects were modeled in Blender with PS1/PS2-era aesthetics — low-poly meshes with hand-mapped textures. Tube TVs, retro consoles, DVDs, lamps, nursery furniture, and fast-food props contribute to the nostalgic atmosphere.
